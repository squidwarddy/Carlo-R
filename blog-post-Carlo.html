Hello! For this blog post I will be analyzing the questions of Jeopardy! Being a big fan of the show, I thought it might be fun to try to unlock the secrets of Jeopardy!’s questions. The data is from r/datasets on reddit (https://www.reddit.com/r/datasets/comments/1uyd0t/200000_jeopardy_questions_in_a_json_file/). It contains about 83% of all Jeopardy! questions ever and it sourced from j-archive.com, an archive of Jeopardy! questions.

There are a ton of ways to go with this data. I am chosing to focus on two research questions. 1: Is there any sentiment related to the questions? Are they positive? Is there one emotion more associated with the questions over another? 2: What topics are these questions covering? History? Sports?

There are numerous other ways I could have taken this project including analysis of the questions over time, how similar questions are to each other, lexical diversity, relationships between question value, round, question length, answer length, etc..

lda_3 <- LDA(dtm_jeo, k = 3, 
           control = list(seed = 123))
topics_3 <- tidy(lda_3,
                 matrix = "beta")
top_terms_3 <- topics_3 %>%
  group_by(topic) %>%
  slice_max(beta, n = 10) %>% 
  ungroup() %>%
  arrange(topic, -beta)

top_terms_3_plot <- top_terms_3 %>%
  mutate(term = reorder_within(term, beta, topic)) %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = beta, y = term, fill = factor(topic))) + 
  geom_col(show.legend = FALSE) +
  facet_wrap(~factor(topic), scales = "free" ) +

gonna stick with 4 topics, seems to have the least overlap between topics & the most encompassing across different topics. That’s why I only labeled the topics for lda_4. Let’s take a look at the gamma for 4 topics to see how often the topics are referred to throughout the Rounds.

jeo_documents <- tidy(lda_4,matrix = "gamma")
jeo_documents %>%

pretty much the same throughout the 4 rounds. Tiebreaker has only happened 4 times so it’s interesting to see that each tiebreaker belongs to 1 of the 4 topics. Also, Final Jeopardy! has more history questions than the other rounds but is still pretty uniform across the rounds and pretty even between the 4 topics.

So, what are our takeaways? The questions are neutral in tone and emotion. Questions are also broken evenly into 4 topics/categories (music, theater, literature and history). This was a little surprising to me. There are questions about sports and inventions and geography in the show. I think the music topic (#1) relates more to modern entertainment (sports, celebrities, music artists). This also just shows how much of jeopardy is more about the liberal arts topics, if you will.
